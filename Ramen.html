Ramen Recipe

Ramen is one of those foods that accidentally became a personality trait for half the planet. It started as a practical noodle soup and somehow turned into everything from a five-minute dorm meal to a dish people line up for at midnight in the rain. At its core, ramen is simple: wheat noodles, broth, and toppings. The magic is in how flexible it is. You can keep it barebones and cheap or stack it with eggs, meat, vegetables, and enough effort to feel like you cooked on purpose. It’s comfort food that doesn’t ask questions, which is honestly rare.

What makes ramen stick around is how it adapts to whoever is eating it. Busy students, exhausted workers, bored home cooks, and actual chefs all use the same basic idea and bend it to their situation. Broth can be light or rich, salty or spicy, and the noodles somehow always feel right. Ramen isn’t fancy by default, but it doesn’t need to be. It shows up when you’re tired, broke, or just craving something warm, and it does its job without judgment. That kind of reliability is hard to beat.

Ingredients

  • 1 pack instant ramen
  • 2 cups water

Instructions

  1. Boil the water.
  2. Add the instant ramen to the boiling water.
  3. Cook for 3 minutes.
  4. Serve and enjoy!
